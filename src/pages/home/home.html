Pharmacy Medicine List

The purpose of this app is to inform the Clinicians of what is routinely stocked by the Avenues Clinic Pharmacy Department. When stocks are running low, the Department will inform the clinical areas as the initial communication. When stocks have run out where we cannot source stock from our wholesalers as they will also be out of stock, we will also communicate via an internal Memo.

  • Stocks which we do not hold in large quantities will be marked with a ***. Before prescribing, the clinician will need to inform the Pharmacist of the impending need so that the patient’s need can be catered for.
  • Stocks which we make in the Pharmacy as a substitute for products that used to be available will be marked with a *.
  • Stock items where we practice Just-In-Time purchasing are marked with ** being items which have a very short shelf life or have an erratic consumption.
  • Stock items which are part of the Private Public Partnership initiatives with the Ministry of Health and Child Care will be marked with a ^^


TB Medication

The Pharmacy Department does not stock TB medication. Patients that need to be initiated are ordered for on the forms provided located in the wards. The pharmacy department will go and collect on behalf of the patient from Wilkins Hospital between 8am and 4pm on week days and between 8am and 12pm on Saturdays. Wilkins Hospital does not provide 2nd Phase medication and insist on the patient relatives to present themselves with the patient’s history in order to receive the medication.



Antiretrovirals (Section 1.5)

The Pharmacy Department’s antiretroviral stocks are limited to Prevention of Mother To Child Transmission (including test kits) and Post Exposure Prophylaxis agents for staff members. It does not stock routine ARVs for patient dispensing. It is encouraged that patient should bring their own ARVs or the relatives arrange for the patient’s own drugs to be given to the nurses. However, if a patient is admitted without their own medication the Clinician should leave a prescription for the Pharmacy to obtain a quotation. Pharmacy will use this quotation to obtain consent from the patient who will then pay for the medication upfront. They will however be receiving a month’s supply, and this can only be done between 8am and 5pm.



Ordering and Sourcing medication outside the list

We encourage Clinicians to consult with the Pharmacists (contacts below) if they need to order medicines outside of this list to ascertain market availability and possible lead time. The lead time for sourcing medication outside of this list is 12-48 hours for products which may be found locally. The Clinician may be asked to fill out a prescription for the patient. Drugs outside this formulary will not be dispensed as TTOs.



Dispensing of To Take Out Medication

When a patient has been discharged, they may need to go home with medication. This medication can be provided by the pharmacy: Between 8am and 3pm on week days and Between 8am and 2pm on weekends.



Emergency Drug Room(EDR)

Please note that the EDR will NOT stock this entire list. Its purpose is to deal with and provide for emergency situations. Please consult the Night Superintendent if there is an absolute dire need for a medicine not stocked in the EDR. EDR list available on the ward.
